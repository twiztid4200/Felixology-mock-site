Felix’s Story

How a glowing book rewrote a town.

Felixology began in an attic above Nason Street, where Libby—then a tired librarian with a box of unshelved donations—found a book that refused to stay closed. Every night, its pages glowed faintly emerald, words rearranging themselves into the exact story she needed to read.

Instead of being afraid, Libby did what any book-lover would do: she made tea, took notes, and followed the book’s quiet instructions. It told her to open a shop where grief, wonder, and local history could sit on the same shelf. It told her to call Noah, Becca, Frankie, and Sarah. It told her to trust that Maynard was ready for a bookstore that felt like a spell.

The glowing book now lives behind the counter, wrapped in linen and cat hair. Some nights, if the jazz is soft and the rain is loud, you can see a faint shimmer through the fabric—like the town itself is still being written.

The 8 Foundations of Felixology

  • Stories choose readers as much as readers choose stories.
  • Grief shelves and joy shelves belong in the same room.
  • Local history is a living spell, not a dusty archive.
  • Every kid who walks in leaves with at least one question answered.
  • Every adult who walks in leaves with at least one question asked.
  • Tea is free, opinions are gentle, recommendations are honest.
  • We stock books that make you feel less alone at 2:17 a.m.
  • Felix the cat is technically “not for sale,” but bribes are considered.

Magical Mind Zine

Send your story into the Felixology field.

Our in-house zine, Magical Mind, collects short stories, poems, comics, and strange little essays from readers near and far. Each year, we bind our favorite pieces into an annual anthology and celebrate with a launch party.

What we love to publish

  • Stories where magic is small, specific, and slightly inconvenient.
  • Poems about rivers, grief, and the weird comfort of fluorescent lights.
  • Comics about cats, librarians, and alternate-universe Maynards.
  • Micro-essays about the book that changed everything for you.

Themed issues, like “Thresholds” and “Attics & Basements,” are announced in advance. Contributors are invited to our annual anthology launch party—complete with readings, live music, and a ceremonial “first glow” of the original Felix book.
