Felix’s Story

The spell at the center of the shop.

Felixology began as a promise whispered in the quiet after unthinkable loss. Felix Brow‑Westbrook was a brilliant, tender, wildly imaginative soul whose worlds spilled across sketchbooks, notebooks, and late‑night conversations. When Felix died, the air around his family felt thin — like the story had been ripped from the page mid‑sentence.

Instead of closing the book, Libby and the people who loved Felix decided to build a new chapter in his honor. Felixology is that chapter: a bookstore that feels like stepping into one of his worlds — mossy, glowing, a little bit haunted, and full of portals. Every shelf, every zine, every art print is a small act of remembrance and rebellion against forgetting.

Here, grief isn’t something to “get over.” It’s something to tend, like a strange, luminous garden. Kids, teens, and adults find books that say, “You’re not the only one.” Artists find walls that say, “Yes, put your weird here.” And somewhere between the stacks, Felix’s presence lingers — not as a ghost, but as a creative force that keeps nudging the story forward.

The 8 Foundations of Felixology

  • 1 Grief is not a problem to solve; it’s a story to honor.
  • 2 Kids deserve spaces that take their inner worlds seriously.
  • 3 Art, books, and slime are equally valid portals.
  • 4 Neurodivergent, queer, and “too much” kids are not side characters.
  • 5 Local history is a spellbook; we’re still writing it.
  • 6 Every purchase helps keep Felix’s story in motion.
  • 7 We are a shop, a shrine, and a studio all at once.
  • 8 Everyone who walks in becomes part of the spell.

Magical Mind Zine

A community spellbook in print.

The Magical Mind Zine is where Felixology spills over the edges of the shop and onto the page. Each issue gathers art, comics, essays, poems, and strange little experiments from kids, teens, and adults who orbit this place.

Some pieces are polished. Some are scribbled. All of them are real. We print in small runs, bind by hand, and celebrate each launch with a tiny party under the reading tree.

  • Themed issues — grief & galaxies, haunted houses, portals, neurospice, and more.
  • Annual anthology — the best pieces from the year, bound into a keepsake volume.
  • Launch parties — readings, signings, slime, and snacks in the shop.
